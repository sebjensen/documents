This week we have been tasked with creating a questionnaire to distribute between and gather information from our target audience. We will be using Google Forms because it integrates directly with our school accounts which we use to host all of our files on Google Drive.

We will be collecting the following information:

We hope to be able to further cater for our target audience by using the data provided from questionnaire responses. The group will be taking an extensive look at the feedback we’ve received in our next meeting.

30th September 2020

Today we held another meeting having just closed responses to our audience questionnaire. Our key findings are as follows:

  • 41 responses were received, with the vast majority falling between the ages 13-16. This is our target demographic.
  • Coldplay was the most recognised artist, along with their song “Paradise”. They were followed by Imagine Dragons and Arctic Monkeys. It would be good to include songs from these top three artists.
  • A multitude of bands and artists in the indie & rock genres were suggested. We’ll be listening to some of the tracks, however, some of them are too guitar-heavy to include (only so much can be reproduced by us in a digital audio workstation).
  • The average preferred concert length was 35 minutes.
  • 85% of respondents were interested in watching the concert live if possible, 95% also wanted to see behind-the-scenes and extra content.
  • YouTube, followed by Instagram, is the most popular social media platform. We will be publish the concert on YouTube, alongside sneak peeks and other updates on Instagram.

We will use this data to ensure our concert appeals to as much of our target demographic as possible.

23rd February 2021

We got the opportunity to come into school and practice in person today. It was great to see Conrad and Owen in person after schools have been shut again for a couple of months, and I think we got a lot from the session. Our work was solely focused on the performance aspect of our virtual concert.

The main benefit from being able to play music together in person was getting the chance to piece together the separate parts we had each been practising. Trying to play in time with each other over video calls doesn’t work due to delays on all ends, syncing separate audio clips in post each time we go to practice together would be impractical. We were able to get some songs sounding really good; the informal audio recordings don’t do it justice.

We were also able to share how we were getting on with our solo performances. For example, Owen’s performance of Song 2 by Blur is sounding great, and we’re near to the stage where we can properly record it. Speaking of Song 2, I’ve found some of the stems for the song, which is good because we can overlay the original guitar track (none of us really play guitar that well, if at all). Even when we were playing by ourselves, it was useful to have the others around to offer feedback.

We’ll continue to practice our parts and hopefully we’ll be able to play together more often once schools eventually re-open.
